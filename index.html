Executive interpretation

Interpretation note: This dashboard combines two live components: FAO ASIS/GIEWS vegetation-health screening and the PNG live processing workspace for drought and frost review. It supports early warning, prioritisation and field verification planning, but does not replace official declarations by mandated national authorities or detailed field assessment by DAL, NARI, NDC, provincial or district teams.

The previous static frost and combined frost+drought products have been removed from the dashboard. The national overview, graphs, tables, map and operational interpretation now update from the latest available FAO ASIS VHI-D dekad where the live data file is available, while the overview also reports the current PNG live processing workspace date and drought/frost analysis windows when that status feed is available.

VHI-D interpretation guide

< 0.35: high vegetation stress.

0.35-0.50: moderate vegetation stress.

0.50-0.65: watch / below-normal vegetation condition.

> 0.65: lower current vegetation stress.

VHI-D is suitable for temporal-discrete vegetation monitoring and provincial zonal statistics. Seasonal ASIS indicators such as ASI and Mean VHI require crop phenology and crop-area logic and should not be casually averaged by calendar month.

Population and livelihood exposure

Change from the previous dashboard: the former population exposure tables were tied to static frost and combined hazard maps. Those have been removed. This tab now provides an ASIS-based exposure planning framework and will display numeric population or household exposure only if those fields are added to the live JSON after overlay analysis.

For a defensible population estimate, intersect live ASIS stress classes with gridded population, census units, agricultural livelihood layers, crop masks and/or community locations. Until that overlay is implemented, the dashboard ranks provinces by current vegetation stress and flags where field verification should be prioritised.

Operational response framework

Field verification approach: Verification should be coordinated through DAL, NARI, NDC, provincial disaster offices, district agriculture officers and relevant partner teams. Verification can use geotagged photos, GPS points, crop condition forms, water-source checks, community/key informant interviews and approved platforms such as KoboToolbox, ODK, Survey123 or other government/partner systems.

ASIS stress groupTrigger from live VHI-DRecommended action
High vegetation stressMean VHI-D below 0.35.Immediate field verification, crop and water-source checks, food security screening, seed-stock protection and early coordination with provincial teams.
Moderate vegetation stressMean VHI-D from 0.35 to below 0.50.Rapid district-level verification, monitor market prices and water availability, prepare advisory messages and check vulnerable crop areas.
Watch / below-normalMean VHI-D from 0.50 to below 0.65.Maintain monitoring, compare with local rainfall reports, watch for worsening vegetation stress and collect community reports.
Lower current stressMean VHI-D at or above 0.65.Routine monitoring and preparedness messaging; verify only where local reports indicate crop or water stress.
